Case Study: Cyberbullying  
Cyberbullying is a type of online intimidation or harassment in which individuals use the internet, social media, or other online platforms to threaten, intimidate, or humiliate others.

The best way to explain this is through the following scenario: A 16-year-old student named Riya posted a picture on social media. A student in her class began commenting on her picture in a derogatory manner. Gradually, others also started commenting and sharing memes and pictures of her. The bully then created a fake social media account and posted pictures and content of Riya in embarrassing manners.  
The progression of the above scenario is as follows:  
(1) Teasing comment on social media,  
(2) Humiliating her in public through social media posts,  
(3) Spreading rumors about her, and  
(4) Creating a fake social media account and humiliating her identity.  
The above scenario is common in cyberbullying, which also includes sending intimidating messages or sharing private information.

Cyberbullying usually happens with teenagers and young adults, and also with people who are vulnerable and have low self-confidence, different looks, and less social support. However, this can also happen with public figures and adults due to the anonymous nature of the internet.  
Cyberbullying can cause serious effects for victims such as: anxiety, depression, isolation, low academic performance and low self-esteem. For some victims, extreme cases have resulted in self-harm or suicidal thoughts. 

Cyberbullying is also continuous and can occur 24/7 which may make it more difficult for a victim to escape compared to traditional bullying. 

The urgency of the situation calls for increased awareness, digital responsibility and strict punishment for online harassment.
